<p><p>Gas Insulated Substations (GIS) are categorized based on voltage levels into two major types: High Voltage and Ultra High Voltage. High Voltage GIS are designed to handle voltages ranging from 72.5 kV to 252 kV, while Ultra High Voltage GIS are able to operate at voltages exceeding 252 kV. Both types of GIS offer compact design, reduced maintenance requirements, and improved safety compared to traditional air-insulated substations, making them ideal for use in densely populated areas or areas with limited space availability.</p></p>
