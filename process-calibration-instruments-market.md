<p><p>Process calibration instruments can be categorized into two main types: benchtop and handheld. Benchtop instruments are larger, stationary devices that are typically used in laboratories or industrial settings where precision and accuracy are essential. They are more suited for calibrating high-end equipment and require a stable, controlled environment. On the other hand, handheld instruments are portable and designed for on-the-go calibration tasks. They are more convenient for fieldwork and can be easily carried around for quick adjustments and troubleshooting.</p></p>
