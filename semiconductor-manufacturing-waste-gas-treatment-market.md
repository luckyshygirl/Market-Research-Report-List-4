<p><p>Semiconductor manufacturing waste gas treatment involves various methods such as direct incineration, activated carbon adsorption, biological oxidation, and others. Direct incineration involves burning the waste gases to eliminate harmful emissions. Activated carbon adsorption uses activated carbon to absorb pollutants from the gas stream. Biological oxidation uses microorganisms to break down contaminants in the gas. Other methods could include catalytic oxidation or scrubbing. Each method has its own advantages and limitations, making them suitable for different types of waste gases in semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
