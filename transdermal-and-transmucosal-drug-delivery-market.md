<p><p>Transdermal drug delivery involves the administration of medication through the skin via patches or creams. This method is advantageous as it provides a controlled release of the drug into the bloodstream, avoiding the need for frequent dosing. On the other hand, transmucosal drug delivery involves the absorption of medication through mucous membranes, such as the oral or nasal mucosa. This route allows for rapid drug absorption and is commonly used for drugs that are not well absorbed orally. Both these methods offer convenient and effective ways to deliver medication to patients.</p></p>
