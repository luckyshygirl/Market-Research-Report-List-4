<p><p>Nurse call information systems are essential tools in healthcare facilities for patients to communicate with nurses. There are two main types of nurse call systems: wired and wireless. Wired systems require physical wiring to connect the patient's call button to the nurse's station, while wireless systems use radio signals to transmit the calls. Wired systems are typically more reliable and cost-effective, while wireless systems offer more flexibility and mobility. Both types play a crucial role in improving communication and response times in healthcare settings.</p></p>
