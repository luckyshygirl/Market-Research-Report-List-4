<p><p>Cell culture media for vaccine production can be categorized into two main types: those containing serum and those that are serum-free. Media containing serum, typically fetal bovine serum, provide essential nutrients and growth factors necessary for cell growth and proliferation. However, serum-free media are becoming increasingly popular due to concerns about contamination and variability. These media are formulated with alternative components such as recombinant proteins, hormones, and growth factors to support cell growth and vaccine production without the need for animal-derived serum.</p></p>
