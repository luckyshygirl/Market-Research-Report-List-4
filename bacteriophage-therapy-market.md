<p><p>Bacteriophage therapy utilizes bacteriophages to treat bacterial infections. The market for this therapy can be divided into three types based on administration routes: oral, topical, and parenteral. Oral bacteriophage therapy involves the ingestion of phage preparations, targeting infections in the gastrointestinal tract. Topical application involves applying phages directly to the skin or mucous membranes to treat infections on the surface. Parenteral administration involves injecting phages directly into the bloodstream to target systemic infections. Each type has its own unique market opportunities and challenges.</p></p>
