<p><p>C-Reactive Protein (CRP) Test is used to measure the levels of CRP in the blood, which can indicate inflammation in the body. The market types for CRP tests include Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA), Immunoturbidimetric assays, Chemiluminescent Immunoassay (CLIA), and others. ELISA is a commonly used method that uses antibodies to detect CRP. Immunoturbidimetric assays measure the turbidity caused by antigen-antibody complexes. CLIA uses chemiluminescent signals to quantify CRP levels. Other methods may include rapid tests or point-of-care tests.</p></p>
