<p><p>Vehicle emission analyzers are used to measure the pollutants emitted from vehicles. The main types of analyzers include non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) analyzers, flame ionization detectors (FID), chemiluminescence analyzers (CLA), constant volume samplers (CVS), and other types. NDIR analyzers use infrared light to detect pollutants, while FID measures hydrocarbons. CLA analyzes pollutants based on their light emission, and CVS collects emissions in a controlled manner. Other types of analyzers may use different technologies or a combination of these methods to measure vehicle emissions accurately.</p></p>
