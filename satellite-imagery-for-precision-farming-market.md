<p><p>Satellite Imagery for Precision Farming offers two main types of imagery: Optical and Radar. Optical imagery captures data based on visible light and can provide detailed images of crops, soil health, and pest infestation. Radar imagery, on the other hand, uses microwave technology to penetrate through clouds and vegetation, allowing for all-weather monitoring of crop health, soil moisture levels, and land topography. Both types of imagery play a crucial role in helping farmers make informed decisions and improve crop yields.</p></p>
