<p><p>Breast localization systems are used in surgical procedures to accurately locate and remove breast tumors. The Wire Guided system involves inserting a thin wire directly into the tumor for guidance during surgery. The Radioisotope system uses a radioactive marker to precisely locate the tumor. The Magnetic Tracer system utilizes magnetic particles to track the tumor's location. Other types of localization systems may use technologies such as ultrasound or infrared imaging. Each system has its own advantages and may be chosen based on the specific needs of the patient and surgeon.</p></p>
