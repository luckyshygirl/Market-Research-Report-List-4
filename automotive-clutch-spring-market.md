<p><p>The automotive clutch spring market consists of various types of springs used in clutch systems, including diaphragm springs, central springs, and other types. Diaphragm springs are the most commonly used type, providing a consistent and uniform pressure on the clutch plate. Central springs are designed to evenly distribute the load across the clutch plate. Other types of clutch springs may include coil springs or wave springs, each offering unique benefits in terms of performance and durability. Overall, these different types cater to a variety of automotive clutch applications.</p></p>
