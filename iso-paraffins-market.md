<p><p>ISO-paraffins are classified based on their carbon chain length. C7-8, C8-9, C10-11, C11-12, C11-13, and C13-14 market types represent different carbon chain lengths within the ISO-paraffin family. Each type has specific uses and applications in various industries such as cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and automotive. C7-8 and C8-9 are typically used for solvents and lubricants, while C10-11 and C11-12 are commonly found in gasoline and diesel fuel additives. C13-14 ISO-paraffins are often used in the production of detergents and surfactants.</p></p>
