<p><ul><li>Lignosulfonates</li><li>Plant Gums and Starches</li><li>Gelatin and Other Hydrocolloids</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Botanic feed binders can be categorized into three main types: lignosulfonates, plant gums and starches, and gelatin and other hydrocolloids. Lignosulfonates are derived from wood pulp and help in binding feed particles together. Plant gums and starches are natural binders made from plants that provide cohesion to feed particles. Gelatin and other hydrocolloids are protein-based binders that improve the texture and stability of feed. Each type offers unique properties and benefits for ensuring effective feed binding in the market.</p></p>
