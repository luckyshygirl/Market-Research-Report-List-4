<p><p>A Saturable Absorber Mirror (SAM) is a crucial device used in mode-locked lasers to achieve ultra-short pulse durations. It consists of a semiconductor saturable absorber layer sandwiched between two mirrors, allowing for the absorption of light intensity to decrease as the intensity increases. <p><strong>The Saturable Absorber Mirror (SAM) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Resonant Saturable Absorber Mirror (RSAM)</li><li>Non-resonant Saturable Absorber Mirror</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Saturable Absorber Mirrors (SAMs) are used in lasers to control their output power. Resonant Saturable Absorber Mirrors (RSAM) are specifically designed to match the resonance frequency of the laser cavity, resulting in more efficient control and modulation. Non-resonant SAMs are more versatile and can be used in a wider range of laser systems, although they may not offer the same level of precision as RSAMs. Both types of SAMs play a crucial role in laser technology and find applications in various industries.</p></p>

<p><p>Saturable Absorber Mirror (SAM) is a device used in various laser applications such as passive mode-locking, Q-switching, and others.
